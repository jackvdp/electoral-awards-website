# Organisational Context

## ICPS

The **International Centre for Parliamentary Studies (ICPS)** builds public-sector capability through training, conferences and policy dialogue. A research institution of the **United Nations Public Administration Network (UNPAN)**, ICPS works with partners such as **UNDP** and **ACEEEO**. All professional courses are accredited by **The Institute of Leadership and Management (TILM)**. ICPS delivers open programmes in London and custom in-house training worldwide.

**Address:** Millbank Tower, London, SW1P 4QP
**Contact:** electoral@parlicentre.org
**Website:** electoralnetwork.org

## Electoral Stakeholders' Network

The Network connects electoral management bodies, civil society organisations, academics, observers, and practitioners worldwide. Its activities include:

- **Annual Awards & Symposium** — the flagship event
- **Bi-monthly webinars** (2+ hours) — deep dives on electoral topics
- **Quarterly regional roundtables** — Africa, Americas, Asia-Pacific, Europe
- **Practice briefs and publications** — knowledge sharing
- **Training programmes** — TILM-accredited courses

## Award Categories (11)

### Nomination Categories (8)
1. **International Institutional Engagement Award** — Honouring election assistance and observation initiatives that increase legitimacy and public confidence in democratic procedures. Open to international institutions, NGOs, CSOs, charities.

2. **Electoral Conflict Management Award** — Recognising effective management of pre/post-electoral conflicts and elections in conflict environments. Open to EMBs, officials, CSOs, NGOs.

3. **Accessibility for All Award** — Initiatives ensuring equal access and participation for marginalised groups (women, persons with disabilities, minorities). Open to EMBs, officials, CSOs, NGOs.

4. **Election Management Award** — Approaches overcoming country-specific challenges to guarantee secure, transparent, smooth elections. Covers voter registration, authentication, logistics, counting, results.

5. **Citizens' Engagement Award** — Putting citizens at the heart of the electoral process. Tackling low turnout, cynicism, distrust, legitimacy issues.

6. **First Time Voter Award** — Outstanding measures to optimise the first-time voter experience. Covers young voters, new citizens, inaugural elections.

7. **Electoral Ergonomy Award** — Tailoring electoral procedures to voter psychology and electorate characteristics. Ballot design, voting machines, polling station organisation, alternative voting mechanisms.

8. **Lifetime Achievement Award** — Remarkable achievements, outstanding work, deep commitment or great sacrifice in elections and democracy.

### Non-Nomination Categories (2)
9. **Electoral Commissioner of the Year** — Selected by the Award Committee based on in-country and international engagement.

10. **Electoral Commission of the Year** — All commissions nominated in other categories are automatically eligible. Selected by the Award Committee.

### Special Awards
11. **Posthumous Meritorious Achievement Award** — Special recognition for deceased individuals.
- **ICPS Excellence Award** (added 2025)
- **Citizen Statesman Award** (added 2024)

## Nomination Criteria (All Categories)
- Originality of the initiative
- Quality of design and implementation
- Effectiveness of results
- Contribution to democratic integrity, innovation, and inclusion

# Webinar Programme (2026)

## Format (120–150 minutes)
1. **Welcome & housekeeping** — 5 mins (host + moderator)
2. **Speakers** — 60–80 mins (3–4 speakers x 20 mins each)
3. **Follow-up questions** — 10 mins (short clarifiers from moderator)
4. **Panel discussion** — 30 mins (all speakers + moderator)
5. **Audience Q&A** — 10–20 mins (chat + hands)
6. **Wrap-up & next steps** — 5 mins (resources, survey, upcoming dates)

## Operational Notes
- Collect 100-word bios and headshots from speakers
- Confirm slide-sharing consent
- Provide captions where possible
- Share reading pack in advance
- Record session; publish edited recording, slides, and key takeaways
- Post-webinar survey
- Bi-monthly cadence
